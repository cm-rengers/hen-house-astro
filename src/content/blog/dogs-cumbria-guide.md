## Why Cumbria is one of the UK's best dog destinations

Few counties in England give dogs, and their owners, more than Cumbria. Between the fells, river valleys and quiet country lanes threading between villages, you will find more fresh-air freedom here than almost anywhere south of the border.

But freedom without safety is not much use to a dog owner. That is where a stay at [The Hen House](/), with its fenced paddock and peaceful rural setting, changes the shape of the trip.

## The fenced paddock: why it matters

Many holiday cottages that say "dog welcome" mean dog tolerated. There may be no secure outdoor space, the garden may back onto a lane, and the nearest off-lead area might be a drive away.

At The Hen House, guests can use an enclosed paddock. Your dog can burn off energy when you arrive, without traffic stress or the usual first-evening hunt for a safe field.

## Dog-friendly walks within easy reach

**Scout Scar, Kendal** is around 15 minutes away and gives you a limestone escarpment with wide views over the Lakeland fells.

**The Lune Valley** offers flatter riverside paths, good for calmer days, older dogs and owners who want a gentler pace.

**The Howgill Fells** suit more adventurous walkers, with open moorland, dramatic skylines and quieter routes than the central Lakes.

**Borrowdale and the southern Lake District** make excellent day trips when you want classic Lakeland scenery, water, woodland and a proper picnic stop.

## Dog-friendly pubs worth the detour

- **The Sun Inn, Kirkby Lonsdale** for a relaxed bar welcome and excellent food.
- **The Punch Bowl, Crosthwaite** for a special meal with dogs welcome in selected areas.
- **The Black Bull, Sedbergh** for a laid-back Dales atmosphere and good local beer.

Always check current dog policies before travelling, especially for restaurant areas and busy weekends.

## What to pack for a Cumbrian dog break

- Towels, more than you think, because rivers and muddy lanes are irresistible.
- A dog first-aid kit for heather, bracken and stony fell paths.
- A long lead for livestock areas and road sections.
- Poo bags in every coat pocket.
- A familiar blanket for settling in after the journey.
